The Methodology

Radical Clarity in Conflict

We don't avoid the "climb." I help you navigate interpersonal challenges by debating until the best solution is found. We treat friction as the grip you need to move higher.

Applied Training

No lectures—only real-time gear checks. I provide specialized tools for high-stakes situations so that when the weather turns, your muscle memory takes over.

The Daily Ascent

Consistency over intensity. I am your Sherpa, providing the daily reps and spotting you every step of the way until you are strong enough to lead the climb yourself.

Your New Title

The Sherpa's KPI

7 Days of Alignment & Accountability

Your first guided ascent. I spot you on every rep until the muscle is built.

Day 1: The Anchor

Link the "What" to the "Why"

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Anchor every tactical task today to the North Star. Don't let the team drift into the "how" until they remember the "why."
06:00 PM The Debrief: Did the anchor hold? If they didn't repeat the strategy back, we increase the weight tomorrow.
Day 2: Route Check

Check for Priority Drift

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Check the map. Ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how clear are you on our #1 priority today?" If it's not a 10, they are climbing the wrong peak.
06:00 PM The Debrief: Did you find drift? Accountability is a course correction before the weather turns.
Day 3: The Grip

Address Misalignment Immediately

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Identify one behavior that doesn't match the strategy. Call it out: "We agreed on Route X, but this looks like Y."
06:00 PM The Debrief: Culture is defined by the worst behavior you are willing to tolerate on the slope.
Day 4: The Metronome

Consistency over Intensity

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Remind the team that climbing is just boring habits done perfectly. Today, praise the process, not just the result.
06:00 PM The Debrief: Did you keep the beat? Your job is to be the steady pulse of the team.
Day 5: The Safety Line

The Ownership Loop

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Every deliverable needs a single throat to choke. Shared responsibility is a frayed rope. Clarify ownership now.
06:00 PM The Debrief: Accountability feels like support when the goals are clear. Did your team feel supported?
Day 6: Handing Over

Teach to Learn

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Today, you step back. Teach a junior leader how to repeat the "Why." Watch them hold the line for the team.
06:00 PM The Debrief: By teaching the route, did you notice a part of your own strategy that you've been neglecting?
Day 7: The Summit

Confirm the Route

08:00 AM The Sherpa's Cue: Review the burn vs. velocity. Prepare your Repeating Script for next week. You are starting to see the mountain like a guide.
06:00 PM The Debrief: The muscle is stronger. Ready for the next ascent?
